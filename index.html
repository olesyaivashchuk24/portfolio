About

My path into embedded systems has been largely self-driven. Since Walsh University offers limited specialized coursework in embedded engineering, I built my experience independently through research-oriented projects involving firmware development, microcontrollers, performance benchmarking, and low-level systems analysis.

I enjoy embedded engineering because it sits at the boundary between software and physical reality — every timing constraint, memory limitation, and microsecond matters.
